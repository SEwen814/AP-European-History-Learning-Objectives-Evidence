🎯 Unit 2 Learning Objectives: Age of Reformation

Protestant Reformation: Causes & Consequences
✓ Analyze the causes and consequences of the Protestant Reformation: Students will be able to identify the key factors that led to the Protestant Reformation and explain its immediate and long-term impacts on European society, politics, and religion.
Protestant Denominations vs. Catholic Doctrine
✓ Compare and contrast the beliefs and practices of various Protestant denominations with those of the Roman Catholic Church: Students will be able to articulate the core tenets of major Protestant movements (e.g., Lutheranism, Calvinism) and differentiate them from Catholic doctrine during the 16th and 17th centuries.
Catholic Reformation / Counter-Reformation
✓ Evaluate the effectiveness of the Catholic Reformation (Counter-Reformation) in responding to Protestant challenges: Students will assess the various strategies employed by the Catholic Church and their impact on maintaining and spreading Catholicism.
Wars of Religion: Religion & Politics
✓ Examine the interplay between religious and political factors during the Wars of Religion: Students will investigate how religious differences fueled political conflicts and how these conflicts, in turn, shaped religious and political landscapes in Europe.
Society, Culture & Art in the Age of Reformation
✓ Assess the impact of the Age of Reformation on European society, culture, and art: Students will analyze how religious and political changes influenced social norms, intellectual developments, and artistic expression during this period.
